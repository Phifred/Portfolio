Why should you play Dungeons and Dragons?

Official Source

To acquire some of the original source material from Wizards of the Coast, such as the starter kit, reference books and more, their website can be found here.

The SRD

For those who don't have much money at their disposal, there is the Fifth Edition Source Reference Document (SRD), a wide-spread, freely available reference that can help people acquaint themselves with the basics, one of the many places to access it is here. It doesn't have the complete coverage of the official Player's Handbook, but it has enough meat for someone to get started. Think of it as the Lite version of the PHB.

No one to play with?

If you're having trouble finding people to put a game together with, don't worry, we can help with that too. Roll20 is a website that gives people a virtual platform to play tabletop RPGs online free of charge. If you're familiar, think of it like Tabletop Simulator, but it's web-based and geared almost exclusively to tabletop RPGs. You just need to create an account and find a game that looks interesting.
